Benefits of exercising

Reducing depression and stress

Physical activity helps distract the mind from life's stresses, which helps avoid negative thoughts. Exercise also helps reduce stress hormones
in the body and stimulates the production of endorphins, which are natural mood enhancers that keep depression and stress away from people.
It also helps increase the feeling of optimism and relaxation after a hard day, and it is worth noting that the relationship between exercise and
depression still needs many studies to prove.

Reducing the risk of contracting some diseases

diabetes by about 30-40%, and exercising helps maintain the health of the bodyâ€™s organs. Preventing high blood pressure, high cholesterol,
osteoporosis, and arthritis.

Providing the body with energy

Exercising for thirty minutes in the morning helps change the whole day; The release of endorphins into the bloodstream during exercise leads to a
feeling of having more energy for the rest of the day. According to Todd Astorino, assistant professor of kinesiology at California State University,
San Marcos, enhancing body strength and endurance makes it easier to complete daily tasks.
