Walking

Walking is a simple activity we do every day. Outside and inside the home. If the body is invited to stay active at home by walking, It is certain that one of them will have a good effect on maintaining physical fitness. This light activity seems trivial, but do not underestimate its benefits to the body if done regularly.

Meditation

Breathing exercises and meditation can make the body and mind more peaceful and calm. naturally, This also needs to be supported by a healthy and hygienic diet as well as ensuring fluid intake into the body to stay hydrated. Of course, the recommended fluid intake by the World Health Organization is mineral water without any added sweeteners so that hydration needs are met.

bike

Cycling boosts your cardiovascular system and enhances overall fitness. Riding a bicycle is an excellent exercise for strengthening the muscles of the legs and buttocks. It helps burn calories and lose weight effectively. It is an environmentally friendly means of transportation that does not cause harmful emissions. It promotes the development of balance and coordination skills

tennis

Tennis requires advanced and fast movements. Which contributes to strengthening the heart and improving blood circulation Tennis requires a variety of movements, acceleration and deceleration. Which enhances general fitness. Tennis requires precise control and eye-hand coordination. Which enhances balance and motor coordination. Tennis involves the use of a wide range of muscles, Especially in the arms and legs. Tennis requires high concentration to follow the ball and make quick decisions. Which enhances the ability to concentrate. Competing in tennis encourages the development of the spirit of competition and patience. Tennis can be a fun social activity that you can play individually or in a group.

Swimming

Swimming strengthens muscles and enhances overall fitness. Swimming strengthens the heart and improves blood circulation, which reduces the risk of heart disease. Floating in the water provides less stress on the joints, making it a suitable sport for people with joint problems. Swimming promotes healthy breathing and strengthens lung capacity. Swimming is a relaxing activity that can help reduce stress and improve your mood. Swimming is an effective way to burn calories, which helps in weight management.

Jump rope

Jumping rope is a comprehensive aerobic exercise that enhances overall fitness and improves the cardiovascular system. Jumping rope activates many muscles, including your legs, arms, shoulders, and abdomen. The jumping rope technique requires precise coordination between the hands, eyes and feet. Which improves balance and motor coordination. Jumping rope is an effective exercise to burn calories, which contributes to weight management.
