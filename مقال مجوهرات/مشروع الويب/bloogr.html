Topaz

Topaz is a precious stone composed of aluminum, silicon, oxygen, and fluorine. It comes in various colors, including golden to deep orange, yellow, yellowish-brown, pink, red, and various shades of blue. Sometimes it appears colorless. Topaz is considered a precious gem in jewelry. The hardness of topaz on the mineral scale is 8. Although topaz can scratch quartz, it cannot scratch ruby, which is only harder than diamond. See: hardness; ruby.

Imperial topaz, with its golden-orange hue, is considered the most valuable type of topaz. On the other hand, colorless and light blue topaz are the most common and affordable varieties. The color intensity of topaz can be manipulated through various techniques, such as heat treatment and irradiation. For instance, certain types of brown topaz can be transformed into pink by heating. Similarly, pale blue topaz can be deepened to a rich blue by a combination of heat and irradiation. Topaz can be found in numerous regions worldwide, most notably in Brazil, Russia, Namibia, Nigeria, Pakistan, and Australia. Brazil produces the finest imperial topaz, and some topaz crystals weighing hundreds of kilograms have been discovered. Possessing large, high-quality topaz gemstones is a point of pride for many museums

Emerald

Emerald belongs to the group of precious stones due to its extreme hardness, but not more than the hardness of ruby. Emerald is characterized by having graded shades of green grass and secondary colors blue and yellow. The reason for the appearance of this wonderful color is that it contains a high percentage of chromium, and therefore if the emerald is exposed to a high temperature, it loses its color. zircon

Zircon is a sub-mineral found all over the world and is very small and very flexible as it can survive in rocks, soil and sediments for billions of years, it is extracted through dry mining methods and wet mining methods. Its colors vary as there is white zircon, blue zircon, red zircon, gray and yellow.
